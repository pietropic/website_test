About Me

I am a PhD student at Sorbonne University (Paris VI) under the supervision of Sébastien Boucksom and Tat Dat To. My current PhD project, as well as my Master thesis, is about Complex Geometry, more precisely the interaction of Non-Archimedian Geometry with Complex Analytic Geometry.

Teaching

In Paris, this year I am teaching two semester courses: Analysis and Topology in R^n, and a Master course on Riemann Surfaces. I have already TA'ed the courses: Analysis and Topology in R^n, and Functional Analysis.

In São Paulo, I served as a teaching assistant for a few undergraduate courses at USP: Calculus II for Engineering, Differentiable Functions and Series, Real Analysis for Math Education and Introduction to Algebraic Topology.

Other teaching and outreach

In all of my undergraduate years I participated at the S4 seminar, which is a student run seminar for freshmen, whose goal is to introduce advanced Mathematics in a simplified way. In 2021, I started an academic group, the A5 Group, which organizes seminars and events for Undergraduate and Master students. Our aim was to introduce research level math for students at IME.
